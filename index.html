02 — Experience

Where I've Made Impact

Constrafor

Head of Platform • DevOps Manager

Current

📍 New York, USA  |  June 2024 – Present

Joining Constrafor meant walking into a fast-moving AI startup and immediately owning the entire platform org — infrastructure, pipelines, security, and the chaos that comes with scaling quickly. My first major push was getting us to SOC 2 Type II, which wasn't just a compliance checkbox — it meant rethinking how we handled access, hardening systems that had grown organically, and building the kind of security culture that actually sticks with engineers.

On the cost side, I tore apart our AWS architecture and found we were paying for a lot of assumptions that no longer held. By restructuring services and rightsizing infrastructure, I brought the monthly cloud bill down by 50% without touching product velocity. Around the same time, I led the re-architecture of our core backend to handle 5x our existing traffic — a project that required close collaboration with engineering leads and a lot of careful tradeoff decisions.

One of the most impactful things I shipped here was migrating our entire CI/CD system from GitLab to GitHub Actions. It wasn't just a tooling switch — we redesigned the entire delivery workflow, and the result was a 70% reduction in deployment time and a release process that engineers actually trust. I also introduced ephemeral PR-based environments, so every pull request gets its own live preview — something that fundamentally changed how the team thinks about code review.

TS Imagine

Cloud DevOps Engineer • Software Engineer

2 years

📍 New York, USA  |  Jan 2022 – Nov 2023

After proving myself in the SRE role, I transitioned into a hybrid DevOps/Software Engineering position where I got to build things from scratch rather than just keeping them alive. The biggest project I led was a proof-of-concept to migrate complex VaR (Value at Risk) financial calculations off aging on-premise hardware and onto GCP. The numbers spoke for themselves — 20% efficiency gains and 30% lower operational costs — and it laid the groundwork for a broader cloud migration strategy.

On the automation side, I built out Jenkins pipelines that handled the full end-to-end deployment of the Risk Product — something the team had previously done manually and inconsistently. I also designed the observability stack using Prometheus and Grafana, which gave the engineering org its first real view into system health across environments. And when it came time to modernize our container infrastructure, I drove the migration from Docker Swarm to GKE — a shift that gave us proper orchestration primitives and made future scaling dramatically cleaner.
